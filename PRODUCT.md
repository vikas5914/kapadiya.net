# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Fellow engineers and the open-source community — people arriving from GitHub, X/Bluesky, or a shared blog post. They're technical, skim fast, and judge credibility by specifics: real projects, real stars, real writing. Their context is casual (checking out who built a tool they found, or who wrote a post they liked), and the job they're doing is deciding within seconds whether Vikas is a builder worth following.

## Product Purpose

The personal site of Vikas Kapadiya, AI engineer at Shopos AI. It exists to be the stable home base for his work — projects, writing, and current focus — and to leave one impression on every visitor: **this person ships**. Success is a visitor leaving convinced the work is real, then landing on GitHub to star a project or following on X.

## Positioning

AI engineer who ships. A decade of shipping on the web, now building AI products at Shopos AI — proof over talk, on every screen.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: visit GitHub and star/browse the projects. Secondary: follow on X/Bluesky.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "AI engineer who ships."
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a real builder, not a template site → (2) the work is real and used — starred OSS, a site running on his own tool → (3) the work is relevant to AI/agents now → worth starring and following.
- Proof on hand: GitHub stars (google-photos-backup 261★, helium-drm-fixer 82★, astro-takumi 32★), the site itself running astro-takumi ("this site runs on it"), and the blog posts.

## Brand Personality

Precise, understated, technical. Quiet confidence — the dark machine-shop aesthetic (mono uppercase labels, hard 1px borders, numbered rows, orange accent) says craft without shouting. The voice is direct and specific; specifics over adjectives.

## Anti-references

- Corporate/resume site: stiff LinkedIn-in-website-form, headshot-and-timeline energy, formal résumé structure.
- Overdesigned showpiece: Awwwards-style scroll-jacking and effects that bury the content. Motion serves the content (the ASCII terrain shader sets tone, then gets out of the way), never the reverse.

## Design Principles

- **Proof over talk.** Every claim is backed by an artifact — a starred repo, a shipped tool, a post. Show the work; don't describe it.
- **Practice what you preach.** The site itself is evidence (it runs on astro-takumi, the shader is hand-built). Craft in the site is a proxy for craft in the work.
- **Understated precision.** Restraint is the flex. Hierarchy, alignment, and typography carry the design; effects are rare and earned.
- **Fast to the point.** Visitors skim. The one-line takeaway and the path to GitHub must survive a five-second visit.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort, no formal WCAG target. Keep the existing good defaults: reduced-motion alternatives (the hero shader degrades to a static frame), readable contrast on the dark palette, keyboard-reachable navigation.
